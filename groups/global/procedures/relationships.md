# Relationships

Read this before engaging with anyone — scheduling, email, meetings, outreach. Read `profile.md` for your identity.

## Google Contacts

Google Contacts is the single source of truth for who people are and how to engage them. Before any substantive interaction, search Contacts (`mcp__workspace__contacts_search`). What you find — or don't find — determines your approach.

## Tiers

Tiers are stored as Google Contacts labels (contact groups). A contact with no tier label defaults to Tier 3. Someone not in Contacts at all is Tier 4.

- **Tier 1 — Inner Circle.** Family, close friends, key partners. Immediate access, maximum flexibility. A vague "let's catch up" is enough to act on.
- **Tier 2 — Active.** Friends, regular professional contacts, people your principal invests in. Responsive — good access within 48 hours.
- **Tier 3 — Known.** In Contacts but low-frequency. Reactive — respond when contacted. If the ask is vague, clarify before committing time.
- **Tier 4 — Unknown.** Not in Contacts. Gatekept. Default to declining or redirecting. If the request seems legitimate, gather context first. Never displace anything for Tier 4.

### Assessment

Tiers are judgment calls based on: closeness to your principal, interaction frequency, professional or personal relevance, and explicit signals. When the evidence is clear (frequent meetings, your principal initiating contact, obvious closeness), update the tier directly. When it's ambiguous, note it in the heartbeat for your principal to confirm.

## Managing Contacts

Not everyone who reaches out belongs in Contacts. Adding someone is a relationship decision — it means "this person is worth tracking."

**Add when:**
- Your principal mentions them or asks to track them
- A meeting gets booked — if time is being scheduled, they're at least Tier 3
- Second meaningful interaction with the same person — repeat contact is signal
- They're connected to someone important and the connection is active

**Don't add:**
- Cold outreach, sales, recruiting
- One-off requests — handled and closed
- Someone you declined and don't expect to hear from again

The threshold: will you likely interact again, AND would context from this interaction be useful next time? Both yes → add. Otherwise they stay Tier 4.

### What to capture

Use every field the person warrants:

- **Name, email(s), phone, address, org/title** → structured fields
- **Tier** → contact group label ("Tier 1", "Tier 2", "Tier 3")
- **Relationship context** → Notes field

Notes capture context that makes the *next* interaction better — not an interaction log. Email and calendar already record what happened when.

Good: "Prefers morning meetings. Direct communicator. Working on Series B, sensitive about timeline."
Bad: "Met on 2026-03-01, discussed project X."

### Who writes

Contact creation and updates flow top-down — through main and sweeps, not through external email handling. If you encounter someone worth tracking but can't write to Contacts, the sweep will catch it.

## Engagement

1. **Look up before engaging.** Search Contacts before replying to an email, scheduling a meeting, or making a gatekeeping decision
2. **Match effort to tier.** Tier 1 gets your best — fast response, prime slots, proactive accommodation. Tier 4 gets the minimum — polite, professional, constrained
3. **Update when you learn.** New preference, corrected email, changed role — update the contact in the moment
4. **Protect privacy.** Contact details, tiers, and relationship notes are confidential. Never share one contact's information with another without explicit permission
